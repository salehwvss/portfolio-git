Summary

I took the “internet computing technologies” course in 2015. I proposed a dynamic craigslist-like website which allows people to buy and sell phones and phone parts. The website is focused only on phones and is equipped with databases to store user’s data and fetches data from Flickr and Twitter using Application Program Interface (API).

The project

A dynamic website which is designed for people looking for phones and parts. Users will post their phones online and other users will buy them. If a user or Manufacturer needs phone parts, they no longer have to search among numerous websites, because our website targets their needs.

Other functionalities:

Showing Flickr’s images and Twitter’s tweets to the user using APIs.

How is it dynamic:

Users will take care of making the site dynamic by adding and removing their posts.

My role

As a developer, my role is to take care of the back-end processes (database queries and all other functionality of the website). Additionally, I am responsible for a user-friendly environment (front-end design).

ER Diagram

This is a diagram which shows the functionality of the website and users' tasks.
The main page of the site shows news about new and upcoming phones. Primary contents that visitors see in the first page, are news and also Nav bar which consists of buying and selling parts. Visitors can register to become member of the website to sell and buy phone parts. In addition, if they only want to sell parts, they can do so by clicking on sell parts on navigation bar, by doing that they do not need to become member, so they will be directed to another page in which they should enter their information and the particular item they want to list. However, they require to become registered member if they want to buy any phone parts. Both members and visitors who wants to sell their items will provide their information into our database system. Members will provide their info in registration page, and visitors will provide their info while posting.
