Project implementation process

We began the process by generating several ideas. We all finally agreed that we wanted to head in the direction of a space-based, puzzle VR experience. Initially, we had chosen the name PHASE based not only off of our initials, but also because our first idea was that the user would be “phasing” through time, they would see the before and after of the space station both intact and destroyed, and would have to prevent the space station from being destroyed. However we eventually decided to ditch the idea and instead had it such that it focuses on the after the space station has already been damaged, and the user has to instead escape. Since we decided to do a puzzle-based game, we split up the work by deciding on a number of puzzles we wanted to implement, and we had decided that we would each work on a puzzle separately. That way we were working in isolation and would be able to join our parts together as needed later on, in a modular way.


Components of the VR application (i.e. 3D assets/models, scripting, audio)

When creating our project, we utilized multiple components which when pieced together, created an experience that allows the player to be immersed in. The first component are 3D models and other 2D assets. Most of our model assets are downloaded from the asset store such as our walls, tables, decorations, and other environmental objects. However, there are some assets that we made ourselves using Maya, Photoshop, After Effects, Pro Tools, and other editing and design software. Notably debris, alarms, graphics for the screens, particles, and other minor models that are used in the background of the scenes were generated by us. Scripting-wise, all of our scripts are made by ourselves with the exception of the oculus VR framework which includes scripts for movement and grabbing. For the audio, we took most of our audio from freesounds.org such as the alarms, music, and other effects. However, there are some that we have made ourselves utilizing the Pro Tools audio software such as the generator sounds..
